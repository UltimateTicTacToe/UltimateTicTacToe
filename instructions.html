How To Play

The game is relatively simple, it's just like Tic-Tac-Toe... except you're playing 9 games... all at once...

The first player may start anywhere in any of the Tic-Tac-Toe games. But note that where you go controls where the next player goes. For example, if you play in the top left box of any of the Tic-Tac-Toe games, the next player must play in the top left Tic-Tac-Toe game. Then if they play in the center box of the top left Tic-Tac-Toe game, you must play your next move in the center Tic-Tac-Toe game.

Once you get three in a row in any of the games, a red letter will be overlaid to indicate that you won that game. Both players may still play in a game that's already been claimed, but once an individual game has been won, the outcome cannot be changed, even if the other player gets three in a row after it's been claimed.

In order to win, you must get three in a row by winning the individual Tic-Tac-Toe games.

Conquest Version

The only difference in the conquest version is that if you get three in a row in a game that's already been won, you take over that game and are declared the winner of that game. So be careful to protect your winnings!
